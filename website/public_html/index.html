About me

I’m currently a doctoral student in the department of computer science at Brunel University where I’m investigating the use of 3D visualisation technology to address the limitations of the existing clinical guidance tools currently in use by older patients and clinicians to perform home-based fall prevention assessments. My doctoral research is funded by EPSRC. Currently writing research papers for peer-reviewed journals in the areas of human-computer-interaction and biomedical health informatics. I have previously been involved in a cyberstalking project led by Professor Carsten Maple where I contributed to the collection and analysis of the survey data from cyberstalking victims.

This research project in particular was reported through the media and resulted in some interesting insights to such an extent that the findings were presented at the house of lord, ultimately led to changes made to the stalking and harassment laws in the UK and recommendations made to the laws in the EU. Additionally I have, in a consultative capacity, advised principle investigators on approaches to undertake multimillion-pound projects submitted as grant proposal applications. I am the Director at Hamm solutions, where I advise, companies from all different sizes, on user experience, software architecture and automation solutions in order to deliver quality software products.
